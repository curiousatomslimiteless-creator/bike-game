Albert Einstein (1879 – 1955)

German-born theoretical physicist widely held to be one of the greatest and most influential scientists of all time. Best known for developing the theory of relativity and major contributions to quantum mechanics. Won the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics.

1. Early Life and Education (1879–1900)

  • Birth: Born into a Jewish family in Ulm, Germany. Moved to Munich shortly after.
  • Speech Delay: Started speaking late, around the age of four, worrying his parents.
  • The Compass: At age five, a pocket compass sparked his lifelong interest in the invisible forces of physics.
  • Schooling: Hated strict teaching styles but excelled at mathematics and physics.
  • Higher Studies: Graduated from Swiss Federal Polytechnic School in Zürich in 1900.

2. The Patent Office & Miracle Year (1901–1905)

  • Employment: Worked as a technical clerk at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern.
  • Marriage: Married college classmate Mileva Marić in 1903. Had three children.
  • The Miracle Year (1905): Published 4 groundbreaking papers: Photoelectric Effect, Brownian Motion, Special Relativity, and Mass-Energy Equivalence ($E=mc^2$).

3. Rise to Global Fame (1906–1932)

  • General Relativity (1915): Showed gravity is the bending of space and time caused by massive objects.
  • Superstardom: Confirmed by British scientists during a solar eclipse in 1919.
  • Nobel Prize: Awarded the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics for the Photoelectric Effect.

4. Escape to America & Later Years (1933–1955)

  • Exile: Fled Nazi Germany in 1933. Settled at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton.
  • Atomic Bomb Letter: Signed a warning letter to President Roosevelt in 1939, later advocating deeply for nuclear disarmament.
  • Passing: Died on 18 April 1955 at age 76 from an abdominal aortic aneurysm.
